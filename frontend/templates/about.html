About Project

Dataset

Based on the UCI Heart Disease schema: each row is one patient with routine clinical features (age, chest pain, blood pressure, cholesterol, ECG, etc.).

The target is a binary label (heart disease: yes / no), and the data is preprocessed as a tabular classification problem.

Project models – base learners

RBM + RF stack (RBM in our naming) learns richer tabular representations, then a random-forest-style head outputs a disease probability from scaled features.

FNN (feedforward neural network) maps the same scaled row through dense layers for a direct neural risk score.

LSTM reshapes the scaled feature vector as a short sequence so a recurrent block can contribute a sequence-style probability alongside the tabular models.

XGBoost supplies a strong gradient-boosted tree probability on the standardized inputs, often paired with LSTM or FNN in hybrids.

Hybrid ensembles (Predict page)

On the Predict screen you choose one of six hybrids: two base scores are fused (fixed weights in the app) into a single risk value. Reported validation accuracies on our setup are shown below for transparency and viva discussion.

  • LSTM + RBM — about 80% validation accuracy.
  • LSTM + FNN — about 78%.
  • LSTM + XGBoost — about 88%.
  • RBM + FNN — about 77%.
  • RBM + XGBoost — about 86%.
  • FNN + XGBoost — about 85%.

Hybrids trade interpretability and compute for robustness: tree-heavy pairs (e.g. with XGBoost) often peak in accuracy, while LSTM+RBM balances recurrent and stacked-tabular views. These figures are from our validation protocol; your own splits or hospitals may differ—always validate before any clinical use.

Real-world use

The system is intended as a decision-support tool that quickly summarizes heart-disease risk from routine clinical inputs to guide further medical evaluation.

In practice, such models can support screening dashboards in clinics, telemedicine triage, and patient education, always alongside clinician judgment.
